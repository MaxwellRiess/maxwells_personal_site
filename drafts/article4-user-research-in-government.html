Recruiting Representative Users

One of the biggest hurdles is finding and recruiting users who are truly representative of the diverse population a government service must serve. This includes individuals with low digital literacy, people with disabilities, and those with limited English proficiency. Outreach has to be proactive and go beyond standard online panels.

From Findings to Action

Presenting research findings is not enough. In a government context, the path from a user insight to a change in a live service is often long and complex. The role of the researcher or product manager is to be a relentless advocate for the user, translating their needs into actionable requirements and demonstrating the tangible benefits of a user-centered approach to skeptical stakeholders.
